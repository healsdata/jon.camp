Using a thought experiment about two PhD students, the author argues that offloading hard cognitive work to AI agents produces competent-looking outputs while hollowing out genuine understanding. Alice struggles through a literature review and builds real intuition. Bob delegates it and gets the same paper. The worry is not that the machines are failing but that we are skipping the parts of learning that actually matter.
