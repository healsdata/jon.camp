David Crawshaw explains why he's launching exe.dev, a new cloud computing platform built on disagreement with how existing providers work. His critique: VMs that bundle fixed resources, remote storage that's both slow and expensive, and egress pricing that punishes growth. He argues that AI-driven software creation makes now the right time to build infrastructure around local NVMe, flexible resource allocation, and straightforward pricing.
