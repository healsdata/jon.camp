Mario Zechner cautions that AI coding agents produce brittle, complex systems when used without careful human oversight. He advocates keeping agents scoped to well-defined tasks, reviewing all generated code yourself, and writing architecture by hand. The piece is a practical argument for slowing down rather than maximizing output.
