Addy Osmani argues that coding agents represent the next step in software's long arc of abstraction, moving developers from writing code to orchestrating fleets of autonomous agents. The factory mental model frames this as building the system that builds software, not just the product itself. Strong specs, clean abstractions, and architectural judgment matter more than ever in this new paradigm.
