A UC Berkeley study of 200 tech workers found that AI tools don't lighten workloads — they intensify them. Employees voluntarily took on broader tasks, blurred work-life boundaries, and juggled more threads simultaneously. What looks like productivity gains can mask cognitive overload and burnout risk.
