Martin Fowler covers three types of system debt (technical, cognitive, and intent) before turning to AI as a "third system" of cognition and the risk of uncritical reliance when humans defer to AI outputs. The core argument is that as coding agents become capable, verification rather than code writing becomes the expensive, high-value work that demands human judgment and reorganized teams.
