The author argues that organizational and personal friction stems from misaligned intentions. Using a travel reimbursement anecdote, he illustrates how shifting from a helpful mindset to an auditing one creates adversarial processes. He warns against installing "internal auditors" in your mind that nitpick progress instead of fostering growth.
