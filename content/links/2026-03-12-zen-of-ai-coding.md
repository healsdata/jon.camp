As AI agents make code cheap to produce, the bottleneck shifts from writing to defining problems and verifying results. The author argues that tight feedback loops, security guardrails, and agent-first product design are the new core competencies for developers who supervise agents rather than type code themselves. Speed without discipline produces output that cannot be trusted.
