The bottleneck in AI-assisted coding isn't Claude's speed, it's the idle time while you wait. The author built jc, a native macOS app with a three-pane layout (terminal, TODO editor, diff view) that lets you manage multiple Claude sessions in parallel. Persistent per-session notes and keyboard-driven navigation between sessions eliminate the context-switching friction that makes parallelism impractical in practice.
